The Early Development Instrument (EDI) is a questionnaire completed by kindergarten teachers in February of the school year for each of the children in their classroom and gathers data about five core areas of development: Physical Health & Well-being, Social Competence, Emotional Maturity, Language and Cognitive Development, and Communication Skills & General Knowledge. The EDI is a nationally and internationally recognized child health monitoring tool used to measure trends and patterns in the developmental health of kindergarten children at the population level.

This online report provides an overview of the EDI data collected across BC during Wave 8 (2019-2022). The report highlights both trends in children’s development over time, from Wave 2 (collected 2004-2007) to Wave 8, and across regions of the province of BC.

Two decades of data coming to life: What we know so far: Still 1 in 3

In Wave 8, 32.9% of kindergarten children in BC – 1 in 3 – are vulnerable on one or more areas critical to their healthy development. This rate represents approximately 13,500 children across the province and is a meaningful increase in EDI vulnerability rates from the Wave 2 (data collected 2004-2007) rate of 29.9%. This level of vulnerability has significant social and economic cost to BC, not simply as children start school but throughout their lives.

Not only has the vulnerability rate in BC risen over the last 2 decades, BC’s rates have been consistently higher than other provinces across the country. “At a national level, vulnerability as measured by the EDI is experienced by 28% of Canadian children.” (NS report…CIHI Canadian #s… https://yourhealthsystem.cihi.ca/hsp/inbrief#!/indicators/013/children-vulnerable-in-areas-of-early-development/;mapC1;mapLevel2;overview;/).

Three key takeaways as a teaser for the detailed picture that follows as possible: Continue to see variability across the province in vulnerability rates and fewer NHs with low vulnerability. Introducing new metrics (on track & in flux) in this wave that help to provide further context on child development trends.

EDI Data Collection

In BC, the Human Early Learning Partnership (HELP, UBC) has been collecting EDI data province-wide since 2001, gathering data for over 330,000 kindergarten children in the last two decades.

EDI data are collected on a three-year schedule called a “Wave” to capture sufficient data in all school districts involved in the province. Data for over 41,000 children attending kindergarten were collected during Wave 8.
